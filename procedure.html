Procedure

A brief breakdown of the experiment's methodology


Step

Description

Image

1

  • The tennis balls first need to be prepared for their respective condtiions.
  • The new balls are kept in their cans and depressurising containers are used to replicate the dead balls for the experiment.
  • A ball dropping test was conducted in order to ensure consistent ball pressures for both conditions.

2

  • The tennis racquet was bound to a steel pull up bar to ensure that the racquet head did not move upon impact.
  • The camera was set up perpendicular to the collision for the best view.
  • The ball machine was set up roughly 20 feet away from the racquet head.

3

  • 30 videos were taken for all four conditions:
  • The 4 conditions:
  1. Vibration dampener with new balls,
  2. Vibration dampener with old balls,
  3. No vibration dampener for new balls,
  4. No vibration dampener for old balls

4

  • The videos clips were then trimmed and cropped to be run through the program.
  • The OpenCV Ball Tracking Program utilized the green contrast of the tennis ball compared to the background in order to track the ball's velocity before and after the collision.
  • The initial and final velocities were used to calculate the coefficient of restitution (COR) in order to compare the experimental conditions.

5

  • A previous study provided a mechanics model of the tennis racquet (treated as a rigid beam) to calculate the force on the hand as a function of the force of the ball (Derived from rotational dynamics).
  • Combined with the impulse equation (Derived from the change in momentum), the force on the hand was calculated as a function of the tennis ball.
  • Parameters such as the dimentions of the racquet, center of mass, COR and impact time were measures or found online to be as precise as possible.
